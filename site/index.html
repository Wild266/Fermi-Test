Project Overview

This honors contract explores whether ensemble-style prompting strategies can evoke Galton's wisdom-of-crowds dynamics in large language models (LLMs) when they solve noisy Fermi estimation tasks.

Benchmark Creation

Designed a 353-question dataset spanning population, energy, infrastructure, and whimsical estimation problems. Each item is annotated with vetted logarithmic answers to enable principled error analysis.

Reasoning Comparisons

Evaluated vanilla Qwen-7B against a chain-of-thought (CoT) variant and a self-consistency ensemble. We measure both exact-order-of-magnitude accuracy and tolerance within ±1 order to capture near misses.

Calibration Research

Inspired by NSF GRFP planning, the project lays groundwork for structured factorization, retrieval-augmented reasoning, and quantifying how impurity scales influence convergence toward correct magnitudes.

Research Goals

Guided by the COMP 691H learning contract and GRFP vision, the research centers on three intersecting thrusts.

Structured Fermi Evaluation

Curate questions with sourced ground truth ranges and unit checks, enabling reproducible benchmarking of LLM estimation skill.

Wisdom-of-Crowds Diagnostics

Probe how sample size, prompt diversity, and self-consistency voting affect log-error shrinkage and convergence behavior.

Calibration & Retrieval

Prototype decomposition strategies, base-rate retrieval, and distributional outputs to mitigate systematic biases in Fermi reasoning.

Experimental Highlights

Baseline vs. chain-of-thought vs. self-consistency ensembles on Qwen-7B (log10 accuracy).

Single Sample Baseline

The vanilla Qwen-7B model answered 34/353 questions exactly (9.6%) and landed within one order of magnitude for 45 questions (12.7%).

CoT Reasoning Boost

Adding chain-of-thought reasoning boosted exact accuracy to 106/353 (30.0%) and ±1 order coverage to 131/353 (37.1%), demonstrating strong benefits from explicit decomposition.

Self-Consistency Runs

Using fermi_direct.py we sample multiple CoT completions and aggregate via majority vote. Collections are ongoing; initial batches indicate further uplift toward Galton-style averaging.

Next Steps

Future experiments will deepen statistical guarantees and expand modalities.

Impurity Scale Sweeps

Systematically vary sampling noise and impurity scales in self-consistency ensembles to measure law-of-large-numbers convergence.

Retrieval-Augmented Estimation

Compare pure reasoning against retrieval-augmented answers to quantify base rate grounding and identify unit-conversion pitfalls.

Calibrated Ranges

Extend the dataset with human-verified confidence intervals, enabling probability coverage evaluation and risk-aware scoring.
